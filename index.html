About Me


I am currently an SoC Architect at Samsung ACL.

I completed my Ph.D in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Nathan Beckmann in the CORGi research group.

Research


My research interests broadly include computer architecture and computer systems, with an emphasis on memory systems and data movement. My PhD dissertation tackled the data-movement challenge by proposing systems that move compute closer to data and data closer to compute. As a research intern at Google, I optimized in-memory caches for miss ratio, I/O cost, and memory usage. And my research in game theory with the Teamcore research group contributed to PAWS: Protection Assistant for Wildlife Security
